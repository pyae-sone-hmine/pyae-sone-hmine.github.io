About Me

I'm a machine learning and robotics engineer studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT. My work spans autonomous systems, reinforcement learning, and control theory. I enjoy building intelligent robots that interact gracefully with the world.

Previously, I've worked at Magna's Advanced Robotics group, MIT CSAIL, the MIT Marine Robotics Lab, and more. At MIT, I teach AI and machine learning to high school students through CogWorks.

Projects

Dexterous Robotic Hand

Overview: Designing and controlling a robotic hand capable of human-like dexterity using reinforcement learning and vision sensors.

Technologies: PyTorch, ROS, OpenCV, Mujoco.

Autonomous Racecar

Overview: Developing an autonomous racing platform with real-time perception, planning, and control modules to compete in autonomous racing challenges.

Technologies: ROS, LiDAR, neural network perception.

BikeLane Monitor

Overview: A system that uses computer vision to monitor bike lanes, detect obstructions, and provide analytics for urban planning.

Technologies: Python, OpenCV, Machine Learning.

Game AI with Reinforcement Learning

Overview: A reinforcement learning agent trained to play strategic games, demonstrating how RL can learn complex policies from simulation.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, Gym.
